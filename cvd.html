CVD Policy

Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure — n8n Flow F10

Maxamin operates a Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure (CVD) process for IoT vulnerabilities discovered during audit engagements. This aligns with NIS2 Article 12 requirements for CVD coordination.

01

Discovery & Documentation

The auditor documents the vulnerability with full reproducibility evidence, CVSS 3.1 scoring, and SHA-256 hashed proof-of-concept artifacts.

02

Client Notification (Day 0)

The client is notified within 24 hours of confirmation. A triage call is scheduled to agree on remediation timeline and disclosure scope.

03

Vendor Notification (Day 7)

If the vulnerability relates to a third-party product, the vendor is notified (with client consent). A 90-day remediation window is granted per industry standard.

04

Remediation Verification (Day 30)

Maxamin re-tests the finding after the client's remediation. A 'Before/After' evidence bundle is produced and hashed.

05

Public Disclosure (Mutual)

Public disclosure occurs only by mutual agreement between Maxamin and the client. No unauthorized publication of client-specific findings.

Reporting a Vulnerability

If you have discovered a security vulnerability in Maxamin's infrastructure or services, please report it responsibly to security@maxamin.io. PGP-encrypted reports are welcome (public key available on request). We will acknowledge receipt within 48 hours.
